Introduction

Clamper Circuit:
A Clamper circuit can be defined as a circuit that shifts the waveform to a desired reference DC level without changing the actual appearance of the input signal. As the DC level gets shifted, a clamper circuit is also called as a Level Shifter.
A Clamper Circuit adds a input AC signal with a DC level. Using the clamping circuits, the peaks (\(+_{ve}\) and \(-_{ve}\)) of the signals can be placed at desired levels.
Clamper circuits consist of energy storage elements like capacitors. A simple clamper circuit comprises of a capacitor, a diode, a resistor and a dc battery if required.


Objective

To study the behaviour of Clamper circuits for following cases.

  • Case 1: Positive clamper
  • Case 2: Positive clamper with positive reference (\( V_r\)) level
  • Case 3: Positive clamper with negative reference (\( V_r\)) level
  • Case 4: Negative clamper
  • Case 5: Negative clamper with positive reference (\( V_r\)) level
  • Case 6: Negative clamper with negative reference (\( V_r\)) level


Theory

Case 1: Positive clamper
  When a negative peak of the signal is clamped above to the zero, then the signal is said to be positively clamped and the circuits is called positive clamper circuit.
  A positive calmper circuit consists of a capacitor (C) in series, reverse biased diode (D) in shunt, and a resistor (R) in shunt as in figure below.
 

  Initially the capacitor is not charged in positive half cycle and the diode is in OFF state. As the capacitor charges to \(-V_m\) in negative half cycle the diode is in ON state.
In the consicuitve positive half cycle, the capacitor is now charged to \(+V_m\) and the diode is in OFF state and gets open circuited. The output of the circuit at this moment will be
 
\( V_o=V_i+V_m \)

  Hence the signal is positively clamped as shown in below figure. The \(V_o\) changes according to the changes in the \(V_i\), but shifts the level according to the charge on the capacitor, as it adds the input \(V_i\).
