Halfwave Rectifier without Filter
Introduction

Almost all electronic circuits require a dc source of power. For portable low power systems batteries may be used. More frequently, however, electronic equipment is energized by a power supply, a piece of equipment which converts the alternating waveform from the power lines into an essentially direct voltage.

Theory

A device, such as the semiconductor diode, which is capable of converting a sinusoidal input waveform (whose average value is zero) into a unidirectional (though not constant) waveform, with a nonzero average component, is called a rectifier.
